## 2. All Insurance Plans

### Plan Categories

Day1Health offers 4 main categories of plans:

1. **Day-to-Day Plans** - For regular doctor visits and medication
2. **Hospital Plans** - For hospital stays and surgeries
3. **Comprehensive Plans** - Complete coverage (day-to-day + hospital)
4. **Senior Plans** - Designed for adults 60+ years

### Plan Variants

Each plan (except Senior) comes in 3 variants:
- **Single** - For 1 adult
- **Couple** - For 2 adults
- **Family** - For multiple adults and children (up to 4 children)

### Plan Tiers

Hospital and Comprehensive plans have 3 tiers:
- **Value Plus** - Essential coverage at affordable prices
- **Platinum** - Enhanced coverage with higher benefit limits
- **Executive** - Premium coverage with maximum benefit limits

**Note**: All tiers have the SAME pricing structure. The difference is in benefit limits, not monthly premiums.

## 5. Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: What is Day1Health?**
A: Day1Health is a South African medical insurance provider offering affordable health cover since 2003. We are underwritten by African Unity Life Ltd (FSP 8447) and provide medical insurance products (not a medical aid). Our plans cover individuals, couples, and families.

**Q: What's the difference between medical insurance and medical aid?**
A: Medical insurance (like Day1Health) provides fixed benefits for medical expenses, while medical aids are registered with the Council for Medical Schemes and offer different benefit structures. Day1Health is a medical insurance product (CMS Ref. DM 1074), not a medical aid. We offer affordable, transparent pricing with clear benefit limits.

**Q: Who can apply for Day1Health?**
A: South African residents and foreign residents can apply. You must be legally residing in South Africa.

**Q: Are there age restrictions?**
A: Regular plans have no upper age limit. Senior plans are designed for adults 60+ years. Children can be added to Family plans (maximum 4 children).

### Coverage Questions

**Q: When does my cover start?**
A: Your cover starts from Day 1 for accidents and emergency ambulance services (no waiting periods). For other benefits, standard waiting periods may apply. Once your application is approved and first payment is received, you can start using your benefits immediately for covered emergencies.

**Q: What are waiting periods?**
A: Waiting periods are times you must wait before certain benefits become available:
- **Accidents & Emergency Ambulance**: No waiting period (Day 1 coverage)
- **General Benefits**: Typically 3 months
- **Pre-existing Conditions**: May have longer waiting periods
- **Specific Procedures**: Varies by procedure

**Q: Can I use any doctor or hospital?**
A: You can use thousands of healthcare providers across South Africa including Life Healthcare, Mediclinic, Clinix, African Health Care, and many GPs and specialists. Visit our website for the full network directory or call 0876 100 600 to check if your preferred provider is in our network.

**Q: What if I need emergency care?**
A: Call our 24/7 emergency hotline at 0861-144-144 immediately. Emergency ambulance services are covered from Day 1 with no waiting periods. For life-threatening emergencies, get to the nearest hospital first, then call us as soon as possible for authorization.

### Family & Dependents

**Q: Can I add my children to my plan?**
A: Yes! Day-to-Day, Hospital, and Comprehensive plans allow you to add up to 4 children. Child rates are:
- Day-to-Day: R193/child
- Hospital: R156/child
- Comprehensive: R266/child

Senior plans do not cover children.

**Q: Can I add my spouse?**
A: Yes! Choose the Couple variant when applying. For Day-to-Day plans, the couple rate is R674/month. For Hospital and Comprehensive plans, it's calculated per adult (R390 or R665 per adult respectively).

**Q: What if I want to add more than 2 adults?**
A: Choose the Family variant. Family plans calculate pricing per adult, so you can add as many adults as needed (plus up to 4 children).

### Application & Changes

**Q: How do I apply for cover?**
A: You can apply in 3 easy ways:
1. **Online** at day1health.co.za (takes 5 minutes)
2. **Call us** at 0876 100 600 (Mon-Fri 8:00-16:30, Sat 8:00-13:00)
3. **Email** sales@day1.co.za for an application form

You'll need your ID, banking details, and medical history information.

**Q: What documents do I need to apply?**
A: You'll need:
- Valid ID or passport
- Proof of residence
- Banking details for debit order
- Medical history questionnaire (we'll provide this)

**Q: Can I upgrade or downgrade my plan?**
A: Yes, you can change your plan at your next renewal date. Contact us at 0876 100 600 or admin@day1.co.za at least 30 days before your renewal to discuss your options. Upgrades may be subject to underwriting and waiting periods for new benefits.

**Q: Can I cancel my plan?**
A: Yes, you can cancel at any time by giving 30 days' written notice. Contact admin@day1.co.za with your cancellation request.

### Claims & Payments

**Q: How do I make a claim?**
A: **For day-to-day benefits**: Simply present your membership card at network providers - they bill us directly.

**For hospital admissions**: Call our emergency hotline 0861-144-144 for pre-authorization.

**For reimbursements**: Complete a claim form (available on our website) and submit with supporting documents to admin@day1.co.za.

**Q: How long does it take to process a claim?**
A: Most claims are processed within 5-10 business days. Emergency claims are prioritized and processed faster.

**Q: What if my claim is rejected?**
A: You'll receive a detailed explanation. You can appeal by contacting admin@day1.co.za with additional information or documentation.

**Q: When are premiums due?**
A: Premiums are collected monthly via debit order on the 1st of each month. Ensure sufficient funds are available.

### Chronic Conditions

**Q: What are chronic conditions and are they covered?**
A: Chronic conditions are long-term health conditions requiring ongoing treatment (e.g., diabetes, hypertension, asthma, high cholesterol). 

**Coverage:**
- **Comprehensive plans**: Include chronic medication benefits
- **Hospital plans**: Cover hospital admissions related to chronic conditions
- **Day-to-Day plans**: Cover acute medication only (not chronic)

Specific limits apply - check your plan details.

**Q: Do I need to register my chronic condition?**
A: Yes, chronic conditions must be registered with Day1Health to receive chronic medication benefits. Contact us at admin@day1.co.za to register.

### Exclusions

**Q: What is not covered?**
A: Common exclusions include:
- Pre-existing conditions (during waiting periods)
- Cosmetic procedures
- Experimental treatments
- Over-the-counter medication (unless prescribed)
- Non-emergency overseas treatment
- Self-inflicted injuries
- Injuries from illegal activities

Each plan has specific exclusions - review your policy document or ask us for details.

## 7. Claims Process

### Day-to-Day Claims

**At Network Providers:**
1. Present your Day1Health membership card
2. Provider verifies your coverage
3. Provider bills Day1Health directly
4. You may pay a co-payment (if applicable)
5. No claim form needed!

**For Reimbursement:**
1. Pay the provider upfront
2. Get a detailed invoice
3. Complete Day1Health reimbursement form
4. Submit to admin@day1.co.za with invoice
5. Claim processed within 5-10 business days
6. Payment made to your bank account

### Hospital Claims

**Pre-Authorization (REQUIRED):**
1. Call 0861-144-144 BEFORE admission (if possible)
2. Provide details: hospital, procedure, doctor
3. Get authorization number
4. Give authorization number to hospital

**Emergency Admissions:**
1. Get to hospital immediately
2. Call 0861-144-144 as soon as possible
3. Provide admission details
4. Authorization processed urgently

**After Discharge:**
1. Hospital bills Day1Health directly
2. You may pay shortfalls (if any)
3. Receive statement of benefits used
4. Keep all documents for records

### Claims Documents Needed

**Day-to-Day Claims:**
- Completed claim form
- Original invoice from provider
- Prescription (for medication claims)
- Proof of payment

**Hospital Claims:**
- Authorization number
- Hospital admission letter
- Discharge summary
- Itemized hospital account
- Specialist reports (if applicable)

### Claims Contact

- **Email**: admin@day1.co.za
- **Phone**: 0876 100 600
- **Emergency**: 0861-144-144 (24/7)
- **Forms**: Download from day1health.co.za/procedures
